What is the best period to travel to Georgia?

[+]
The best time to visit Georgia is during spring (April-May) and autumn (September-October) when the weather is mild and pleasant. Summer (June-August) is also popular for tourism, though it can be hot. Winter (December-February) is suitable for skiing in the mountains, while spring offers beautiful wildflowers and mild temperatures perfect for trekking and sightseeing.

Do I need a visa to visit Georgia?

[+]
Most visitors do not need a visa to enter Georgia. Citizens of many countries, including EU, US, UK, Canada, and Australia, can enter visa-free for up to 365 days. However, visa requirements vary by nationality, so we recommend checking the Georgian government's official website or contacting your nearest Georgian embassy for your specific country's requirements before your trip.

What holidays are celebrated in Georgia?

[+]
Georgia celebrates several important holidays including New Year (January 1), Orthodox Christmas (January 7), Women's Day (March 8), Independence Day (May 26), and Assumption of Mary (August 28). Religious holidays like Easter and various saint days are also observed. These celebrations offer visitors unique cultural experiences and insights into Georgian traditions and spirituality.

What is the official currency of Georgia?

[+]
The official currency of Georgia is the Georgian Lari (GEL). It is recommended to carry some local currency for small purchases, though major credit cards are accepted in most hotels, restaurants, and tourist attractions in larger cities. ATMs are widely available throughout the country, and currency exchange services are readily accessible.

What payment methods are accepted in Georgia?

[+]
In Georgia, cash (Georgian Lari) is still widely used, especially in smaller towns and rural areas. Major credit cards (Visa, MasterCard) are accepted in hotels, restaurants, and shops in Tbilisi and larger cities. ATMs are readily available for cash withdrawals. We recommend informing your bank of your travel dates to avoid card blocks, and carrying some cash for smaller establishments.
